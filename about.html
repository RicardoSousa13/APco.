Albuquerque Pinho and Co is a collaborative architecture and design studio founded in 2020 by Luís Albuquerque Pinho. Based in Porto, the studio works across the fields of architecture, object design, wellbeing, critical thinking, and aesthetics, performing nationally and internationally. Its multidisciplinary practice embraces design as a cultural and ethical act — shaping not only the physical space, but also the relational spheres and ecological landscapes.
With a primary focus on housing, public cultural buildings and urban planning, the studio’s work is guided by a strong ethos of generosity and economy. Each project aspires to serve life and community, foster use and appropriation, challenging the conventional norms through precise yet open-ended design strategies, placing the principles of sustainability at the core if its designs and philosophy. Through the selection of materials, construction methods, and energy use, the studio’s work reflects a commitment to architecture’s role in shaping climate-conscious and regenerative futures.
Beyond architectural practice, Albuquerque Pinho and Co embraces an expanded practice, collaborating across disciplines and media, with projects for exhibition displays for Galeria Vertical at Silo-Auto in Porto (2016), a public installation for the 20th anniversary of Porto’s as a UNESCO World Heritage Site (2016), and also collaborated with the Portuguese artist Carla Filipe on a project for a garden installation for the Ping Yao Sculpture Park in China (2019). These projects reflect the studio’s commitment to cultural engagement and experimentation, pushing architecture’s boundaries into art, public space, and collective memory.

Luís Albuquerque Pinho
Architect, Curator, and Researcher

Luís Albuquerque Pinho is a Portuguese architect and curator, born in Porto in 1978. He obtained his degree in Architecture from Universidade Lusíada do Porto in 2001. Between 2000 and 2011, he collaborated with architects Isabel Furtado and João Pedro Serôdio. From 2001 to 2019, he was associate architect at Garcia e Albuquerque.
Expanding his expertise into the fields of art and curation Luis Albuquerque Pinho enrolled postgraduate studies in Art Studies — Museum and Curatorial Studies at the Faculty of Fine Arts, University of Porto (FBAUP). In 2011, he was a resident curator at the Node Center for Curatorial Studies in Berlin and participated in the Independent Study Programme at Escola Maumaus in Lisbon.
Since 2011, he has developed independent curatorial and research projects in collaboration with Luís Pinto Nunes. Notably, they co-edited the publication PARQUE — Conjunto Habitacional Luso-Lima 1958—2023, contributing to the documentation of this architectural heritage.
In 2021, Luis Albuquerque Pinho was appointed as a jury member for the SHUTTLE grants, a program by Porto City Hall (Pláka) supporting the internationalisation of artistic practices. Since 2020, he leads his own practice, Albuquerque Pinho and Co, focusing on architecture and consulting, with an emphasis on sustainability and the integration of traditional construction methods.

Albuquerque Pinho and Co continues to evolve as a critical and creative platform, committed to developing architectural practices that are not only intellectually and socially rigorous, but also deeply responsive to the environmental challenges of our time.
